<h1 class="page-topper__title">
<span class="visually-hidden">
The MIT education: 
</span>
Majors &#038; minors
</h1>
\n
</div>
\n
</div>
\n
</div>
\n
<article class="page__body">
\n
<div id="page-text-mod" class="page__text-mod">
\n
<p class="intro-text">
At MIT, majors are conventionally called courses, and they&#8217;re numbered rather than named; meanwhile, our credits are called units and they&#8217;re counted differently than at most other universities. The terminology can be confusing, but the important thing to know is that we have many things you can learn through programs that are at the leading edge of their field.
</p>
\n
<h3>
Your first year
</h3>
\n
<p>
When you apply to MIT, you apply to the entire university, not to a specific major or school, so all 
<a href="https://mitadmissions.org/discover/the-mit-education/the-first-year/">
first-year students
</a>
 begin MIT undeclared. During your first year, MIT will provide academic fairs, lectures, seminars, and other programs to help you determine which major will suit you best; you are then free to choose from among any of MIT&#8217;s courses of study, without any additional requirements or admission procedures.
</p>
\n
<h3>
Our schools
</h3>
\n
<p>
MIT is organized into several schools of study:
</p>
\n
<ul>
\n
<li>
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/schools/architecture-planning/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
School of Architecture and Planning
</a>
</li>
\n
<li>
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/schools/engineering/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
School of Engineering
</a>
</li>
\n
<li>
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/schools/humanities-arts-social-sciences/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
School of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences
</a>
</li>
\n
<li>
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/schools/sloan-management/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
MIT Sloan School of Management
</a>
</li>
\n
<li>
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/schools/science/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
School of Science
</a>
</li>
\n
</ul>
\n
<p>
We also have the 
<a href="http://computing.mit.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
Schwarzman College of Computing
</a>
 that coordinates computing education, research, and infrastructure across the schools.
</p>
\n
<p>
Each of these schools offers 
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/degree-charts/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
degrees
</a>
\xc2\xa0in their courses of study, as well as 
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/mit/undergraduate-education/academic-programs/minors/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
minors
</a>
 and/or 
<a href="http://web.mit.edu/hassreq/concentrations.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
concentrations,
</a>
\xc2\xa0and teach 
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/subjects/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
thousands of classes
</a>
\xc2\xa0open to any student no matter their course of study or departmental affiliation. Students may complete a traditional degree, an\xc2\xa0
<a href="http://catalog.mit.edu/interdisciplinary/undergraduate-programs/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
interdisciplinary degree
</a>
, a 
<a href="https://shass.mit.edu/undergraduate/joint">
joint degree
</a>
, or a 
<a href="http://web.mit.edu/registrar/subjects/cmtes/coc/DM-FAQ.html#what" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">
double-major
</a>
. However, at MIT, the focus is less on credentialing and more on the substance of what you need to learn to effectively solve problems that matter to you.
</p>
\n
